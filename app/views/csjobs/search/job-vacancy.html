About the job

Job summary

The Social Media Manager is an important role which is responsible for planning and producing powerful and compelling digital content that supports our campaigns across a range of platforms. No two days will be the same and you will be responsible for working on important issues and announcements that affect our environment and society. This hands-on role will be responsible for creating and managing digital creative content and campaigns and telling our story to online audiences.

Job description

  • Produce expert-standard social content, optimised for different channels, such as statics, videos, animations, Instagram stories, Reels and quizzes.
  • Weave digital storytelling into innovative content for owned channels as well as for external partner channels, writing storyboards for video and animation content.
  • Taking part in an incident rota as required, which may involve working outside office hours and on weekends.
  • Create incident monitoring reports using digital tools such as Brandwatch.
  • Write evaluation reports which include actionable insights for future content.

Person specification

  • Current experience of leading social media channel management, including professional tools as well as using systems to provide issue monitoring, reporting, rebuttal advice and evaluation for the business.
  • Proven experience of identifying and managing social media collaborations and partnerships
  • Excellent written and oral communications skills reflecting the need to set standards for communication and provide editorial control to ensure appropriate content, messaging, tone and consistency in all communications.
  • The ability to work well under pressure and manage multiple priorities and tight deadlines and carry others with them under such circumstances.
  • Experience working in fast-paced environments, leading on social media communications.
  • An excellent understanding of social media trends and audiences.

Selection process details

Application Process

As part of the application process you will be asked to submit information about your skills and experience, a personal statement and Civil Service Behaviour statements.

Further details around what this will entail are listed on the application form.

Sift

For your personal statement, please provide detailed evidence of the criteria in the person specification. Should a large number of applications be received, an initial sift may be conducted using the personal statement. Candidates who pass the initial sift may be progressed to a full sift, or progressed straight to assessment/interview.

Sift and interview dates, as well as the interview location, to be confirmed.

Interview

If successful at application stage, you will be invited to interview where you will be assessed on the following Civil Service Behaviours: Communicating and Influencing, Delivering at Pace and Working Together; the following Technical Skill: Digital Skills; and a Strength.

Internal Fraud Database Check

Applicants who are successful at interview will be, as part of pre-employment screening subject to a check on the Internal Fraud Database (IFD). This check will provide information about employees who have been dismissed for fraud or dishonesty offences. This check also applies to employees who resign or otherwise leave before being dismissed for fraud or dishonesty had their employment continued. Any applicant’s details held on the IFD will be refused employment.

A candidate is not eligible to apply for a role within the Civil Service if the application is made within a 5-year period following a dismissal for carrying out internal fraud against government.
