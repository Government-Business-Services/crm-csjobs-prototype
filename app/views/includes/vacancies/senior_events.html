About the job

Job summary

We are looking for an ambitious and proven communications professional to support Defra’s creative and highly-respected Events and Visits team.

The postholder will oversee the continued delivery of high-profile events and visits linked to the Secretary of State’s key policy areas including improving and protecting the environment, growing a green economy, sustaining thriving rural communities and supporting our world-leading food, farming, and fishing industries.

This role requires travel within the UK (and may include occasional overseas travel).

Job description

  • Work as part of a talented team of people overseeing external engagement activity and Ministerial visits across the Department.
  • Work collaboratively with other comms pillars and policy teams to ensure that there is a consistent approach to all stakeholder events and visits.
  • Collaborate and build relationships with our external partners in order to facilitate high quality visits and events including agencies, photographers, businesses and other government departments.
  • Build strong relationships with Special Advisers (SpAds) and Private Offices and become a trusted adviser of outreach activity.
  • Line manage two IOs and ensure they have stretching goals and development plans.
  • Drafting detailed itineraries and producing high quality briefings for events and visits working in partnership with policy teams.
  • Advising on stakeholder engagement and communications, and of building excellent networks.
  • Working closely with the Secretary of State, Junior Ministers, Special Advisers, officials and external partners.

Person specification

  • Experience working with press office, digital and strategic comms to deliver high quality work, often at pace.
  • Detailed understanding of Westminster, and of political process in the UK and Devolved Administrations.
  • Demonstrable knowledge and understanding of Defra topics and a track record of working with stakeholders.
  • Ability to work at pace and to adapt to changing scenarios.

The successful candidate will require CTC clearance. Therefore, confirmation of appointment to the advertised post will be dependent upon successful CTC clearance being confirmed as part of pre-employment checks.

Internal Fraud Database Check

Applicants who are successful at interview will be, as part of pre-employment screening subject to a check on the Internal Fraud Database (IFD). This check will provide information about employees who have been dismissed for fraud or dishonesty offences. This check also applies to employees who resign or otherwise leave before being dismissed for fraud or dishonesty had their employment continued. Any applicant’s details held on the IFD will be refused employment.

A candidate is not eligible to apply for a role within the Civil Service if the application is made within a 5-year period following a dismissal for carrying out internal fraud against government.
