About the job

Job summary

Do you have an interest in criminal law and are looking to become a fully qualified solicitor?

Apply for the Crown Prosecution Service (CPS) legal trainee scheme now and start your training pathway in October 2025.

We currently have two adverts live, one for the barrister trainee role and one for the solicitor role. You can only apply for one of these vacancies. If you apply for both positions, we will only accept an application for the first role you applied for and will automatically reject the second. However, if this has been an error and you wish for the second application to be considered, email earlycareers@cps.gov.uk by 1pm on Wednesday 7 February 2024.

The pathway is open to you if you hold an undergraduate degree in law (or have completed a conversion) at a minimum 2:2 and have completed, or will be due to complete, your postgraduate Legal Practice Course (LPC) or SQE 1 and 2 so you can start training in October 2025.

We are now also inviting applications if you’re a final-year law undergraduate completing a law conversion course with a predicted minimum 2:2, and will have completed your LPC or SQE 1 and 2 to start training in October 2025.

We’re offering up to 80 legal trainee posts for both solicitors and barristers in CPS locations across England and Wales. These are full-time posts although flexible and part-time working is considered, taking into account business needs.

As a legal trainee solicitor, you play a key role in the CPS. You support our duty to ensure the right person is prosecuted for the right offence, bringing offenders to justice wherever possible.

Find out what our current legal trainees say about working at the CPS, cps.gov.uk/careers/legal-trainee.

Please note that the number of posts available is subject to change and that final offers will reflect business needs.

Solicitor training is available, with posts offered in order of merit to the top performing candidates, subject to having appropriate supervisory arrangements in place. Once you’ve successfully completed the CPS legal trainee scheme, you become a permanent crown prosecutor.

These posts are available nationally. You’re asked to confirm the location you would prefer to be based. Offers are made in merit order in line with your location preference order. It isn’t guaranteed that your location preference will be accommodated.

You must complete and pass your Civil Service tests by 1pm on Tuesday 6 February 2024 to be able to complete an application for this role.

Costs incurred for the Professional Skills Course are covered by the CPS.

Job description

As a legal trainee solicitor, you complete your studies and train to become a fully qualified solicitor. You’re supported by your legal trainee supervisor, gaining knowledge, experience, an understanding of working as a prosecutor within the CPS and how we work with other criminal justice partners.

Once you successfully qualify, you’re offered a permanent role as a crown prosecutor, working in a magistrates’ court unit with fellow prosecutors and caseworkers. We offer great progression opportunities – you have the chance to work in specialist crime areas including rape, serious sexual offences and fraud. You can also consider a move into management and leadership, look to be promoted to senior crown prosecutor, and into crown advocacy, presenting cases in the Crown Court.

Opportunities to apply for roles within the CPS and develop your career after the completion of the training course will be available.

Person specification

  • To prepare cases for prosecution on summary-only and either-way offences in the magistrates’ court. Working with caseworkers, meeting witnesses, and supporting counsel.
  • To advise police and other investigators on charging decisions, and work with criminal justice partners, defence lawyers and counsel on casework.
  • To shadow prosecutors at magistrates’ and Crown Court and observe advocacy.
  • To attend conferences with police and counsel, and meetings with victims and witnesses.
  • To carry out legal research and provide feedback to your legal supervisor and other CPS colleagues.
  • To work with a supervisor throughout pupillage, keeping accurate records of work and meeting to discuss training progress.
  • To arrange and complete your required work secondments, in line with the Law Society.

Qualifications

Undergraduate degree

You must hold a minimum of a 2:2 university undergraduate degree – this doesn’t need to be in law. Equivalent undergraduate degrees are considered.

If you have a university undergraduate degree at a minimum of a 2:2 in a subject other than law, you must have completed one of the following conversion courses:

  • Graduate Diploma in Law (GDL)
  • Common Professional Examination (CPE)

Postgraduate qualifications

You must have successfully completed or be due to complete the Law Practice Course (LPC) or SQE to be able to start with the CPS by October 2025. And have passed the LPC or both parts of the SQE (SQE1 and SQE2) by the time you join the scheme.

Qualifications must be successfully completed, with results provided to the CPS recruitment team by 1 September 2025 at the latest. If you’re unable to provide confirmation of passing your LPC or SQE by this time, you are removed from the process.

We don’t accept CILEX instead of the LPC or SQE qualification.

Internal Fraud Database Check

Applicants who are successful at interview will be, as part of pre-employment screening subject to a check on the Internal Fraud Database (IFD). This check will provide information about employees who have been dismissed for fraud or dishonesty offences. This check also applies to employees who resign or otherwise leave before being dismissed for fraud or dishonesty had their employment continued. Any applicant’s details held on the IFD will be refused employment.

A candidate is not eligible to apply for a role within the Civil Service if the application is made within a 5-year period following a dismissal for carrying out internal fraud against government.
